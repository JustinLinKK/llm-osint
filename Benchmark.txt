## Publication Record and Research Expertise
The publication record of Xinyu Pi offers the most comprehensive insight into his specialized expertise, which lies at the intersection of Artificial Intelligence, Natural Language Processing (NLP), and Logical Reasoning. His body of work is prolific and centers on addressing the fundamental limitations of Large Language Models (LLMs), particularly their tendency to produce plausible but incorrect outputs and their lack of verifiable reasoning processes. His research can be broadly categorized into three interconnected themes: enhancing logical reasoning capabilities, improving model robustness and faithfulness, and applying LLMs to qualitative data analysis.
A central pillar of his research is "Reasoning Like Program Executors," a line of work that explores methods to make LLMs perform complex reasoning tasks with greater accuracy by simulating the step-by-step execution of code [[52](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Reasoning-Like-Program-Executors-Pi-Liu/03488f1a193066b5ea8b9b800e119f07df5c1d9e), [61](https://arxiv.org/html/2502.19411v1), [93](https://aclanthology.org/2022.emnlp-main.48/), [184](https://webdocs.cs.ualberta.ca/~dale/papers/neurips22a.pdf)]. This approach treats the problem of reasoning as analogous to program execution, thereby grounding abstract language understanding in a more deterministic process. Building on this theme, he has developed models like LogiGAN, which learns logical reasoning through adversarial pre-training, effectively teaching the model to distinguish between valid and invalid logical inferences [[133](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Disentangling-Reasoning-Capabilities-from-Language-Zhong-Ma/70f1419c0778350abb75ff884691b9933e408888), [134](https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Improving-Commonsense-Causal-Reasoning-by-Training-Staliunaite-Gorinski/be5c4cbde12db1c7bb89e3775e41e207aa4f9ed3), [164](https://pdfs.semanticscholar.org/ed4e/0ed200aa903002f862122c15dd96186c604c.pdf), [182](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/381830607_Abstract_Meaning_Representation-Based_Logic-Driven_Data_Augmentation_for_Logical_Reasoning)]. Other works in this vein focus on infusing logical structures directly into pre-trained language models to improve their performance on reasoning tasks [[182](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/381830607_Abstract_Meaning_Representation-Based_Logic-Driven_Data_Augmentation_for_Logical_Reasoning), [185](https://aclanthology.org/2024.acl-long.531.pdf)].
